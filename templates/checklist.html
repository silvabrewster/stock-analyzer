Before acting on any pick — answer these questions honestly. High score ≠ guaranteed return.

🔴 Must Answer — Business Understanding
Do I understand how this company makes money? Must
Can you explain their business model in one sentence? If not, don't invest — Warren Buffett's rule.
What are the 2-3 biggest risks to this business? Must
Regulation changes, competition, economic sensitivity, debt levels. If you can't name them, you haven't researched enough.
Can I hold this for at least 3-5 years without panic selling? Must
Most stocks drop 20-40% at some point. If you'd sell at a 30% loss, you're not ready to own it.
Is this money I won't need for 3+ years? Must
Never invest money you might need for rent, emergencies, or near-term expenses.
🟡 Important — Valuation & Financials
Is the stock reasonably valued vs its earnings growth? Important
Check the PEG ratio (P/E divided by growth rate). Under 1.5 = reasonable. Over 3 = expensive. A high score doesn't mean cheap.
Is revenue and earnings growing year over year? Important
Check the last 3 years of financials on Yahoo Finance → Financials tab. Consistent growth = healthy business.
Is the debt level manageable? Important
Debt-to-equity under 1.0 is generally safe. High debt + rising interest rates = danger. Check Yahoo Finance → Balance Sheet.
Does the company generate positive free cash flow? Important
Free cash flow = real money left after expenses. Companies burning cash are risky. Check Yahoo Finance → Cash Flow.
How does it compare to competitors in the same sector? Important
Is it the market leader or a weaker player? Morningstar's moat rating helps here. Leaders tend to stay leaders.
🔵 Nice to Know — Conviction Boosters
Has this stock appeared in the top 10 for 3+ consecutive days? Nice
Check the Streak column in the dashboard. Persistent signals are more reliable than one-day spikes.
Are insiders (CEO, CFO) buying shares recently? Nice
Check openinsider.com for the ticker. Cluster buying = management believes in the stock. Selling is less meaningful.
Is the stock beating the S&P 500 over the last 3-6 months? Nice
Relative strength matters — strong stocks tend to stay strong. Check the "Beats S&P" column in the dashboard.
Does the overall market environment support this investment? Nice
Check the market bar: VIX above 30 = high fear/volatility. 10yr yield above 5% = headwind for growth stocks. Context matters.
Am I diversified — not putting too much in one stock or sector? Nice
No single stock should be more than 5-10% of your portfolio. Check sector concentration warning in the dashboard.
